## What Information You Get

When dispatch notification appears, you immediately receive:

- **Emergency ID**: Call reference number
- **Emergency Type**: Trauma, Medical, Psychiatric, etc.
- **Location**: Where patient is located
- **Priority Level**: CRITICAL, HIGH, MEDIUM, LOW
- **Patient Name**: Person needing help
- **Patient Age**: For medical assessment
- **Patient Condition**: What's wrong (injury/symptoms)
- **Hospital Destination**: Where patient going
- **Dispatcher Name**: Who assigned you
- **Dispatcher Phone**: Direct contact number

## Notification Details

### Visual Alert
- **Color**: Green (success/action required)
- **Position**: Top of screen
- **Size**: 500px wide on desktop, responsive on mobile
- **Auto-Close**: 3 seconds (manual close available)
- **Sound**: Yes, 800Hz tone (if unmuted)

### Audio Notification
- **Type**: Sine wave tone
- **Frequency**: 800 Hz (medical alert standard)
- **Duration**: 0.5 seconds
- **Volume**: 30% (not jarring, but clear)
- **Device**: Plays through your device speakers
- **Mute**: Mutes with browser audio settings

### Auto-Reload
- **Timing**: 2-3 seconds after notification dismissed
- **What Updates**: Dashboard refreshes with call data
- **Data**: Shows complete patient and preparation info
- **Manual**: Can manually refresh browser if needed

## FAQ

**Q: What if I miss the notification?**
A: Check dashboard - shows active call and preparation tasks. Click refresh if needed.

**Q: Can I silence the audio?**
A: Yes - mute browser or individual page audio. Visual notification still shows.

**Q: How long do I have to prepare?**
A: Depends on distance. Usually 4-10 minutes. Dashboard shows timestamp for tracking.

**Q: What if I'm still preparing when ambulance arrives?**
A: Do your best. Focus on safety-critical items first (bed, equipment, staff notify).

**Q: Can I cancel a dispatch?**
A: No. Contact dispatcher immediately by phone if there's an issue.

**Q: What if patient info is wrong?**
A: Contact dispatcher immediately. You can update notes in dashboard.

**Q: Where's my active call info?**
A: Dashboard shows all details: patient info, location, hospital, timeline.

**Q: How do I know ambulance is coming?**
A: Check Response Timeline - shows DISPATCHED status with timestamp.

**Q: What's the preparation checklist for?**
A: Specific hospital tasks to prepare before patient arrives.

**Q: Do I need to confirm I got the dispatch?**
A: No, system auto-confirms when you get notification. Just start preparation.

## Common Preparation Scenarios

### Scenario: Trauma/Accident
**Your Task**: Be ready for injuries
- Bed with trauma setup
- Monitoring equipment connected
- Trauma team notified
- Trauma medications prepared
- Surgery team alerted if needed

### Scenario: Cardiac Emergency  
**Your Task**: Be ready for heart issues
- Monitored bed ready
- ECG leads prepared
- Cardiology team notified
- Cardiac medications staged
- Defibrillator nearby

### Scenario: Stroke
**Your Task**: Be ready for time-sensitive emergency
- Isolation room available
- Neurology notified
- CT/MRI pre-registered
- Medications ready
- Protocol initiation prepared

### Scenario: Medical (Non-Trauma)
**Your Task**: Depend on condition
- Appropriate bed level
- Relevant staff notified
- Condition-specific equipment
- Medications matching diagnosis
- Isolation if infectious

## Staff Notification Protocol

When you notify staff:
1. **Patient Name**: Who's arriving
2. **Condition**: What's wrong
3. **ETA**: When ambulance arriving
4. **Requirements**: Special needs
5. **Bed Location**: Where patient going

Example: *"Incoming trauma patient John Doe, 45M with severe head injury, ETA 5 minutes. Ward 3, trauma protocol. Dr. Ahmed alerted."*

## Before Ambulance Arrives

**5 Minutes Before**:
- Bed fully prepared and clean
- Equipment tested and ready
- Staff in positions
- Medications accessible
- Documentation forms ready

**2 Minutes Before**:
- Final equipment check
- Staff standing by
- Doors/gates opened
- Lighting adequate
- Transport route clear

**Ambulance Arriving**:
- Team ready at door
- Bed easily accessible
- Monitors ready to connect
- Handoff procedure prepared
